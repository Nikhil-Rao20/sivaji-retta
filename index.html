Research

My research interests lies in Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Computer Vision, Image Processing, NLP and Time Series Analysis


CattleEyeView: A Multi-task Top-down View Cattle Dataset for Smarter Precision Livestock Farming

KE Ong, Sivaji Retta, R Srinivasan, S Tan, J Liu
2023 IEEE International Conference on Visual Communications and Image Processing (VCIP), 2023
IEEE / Google Scholar
We propose CattleEyeView, a comprehensive dataset for precision livestock farming, focusing on multi-task learning in a top-down view setting, improving cattle monitoring and management.

Comprehensive Analysis of Deep Learning Approaches for PM2. 5 Forecasting

Sivaji Retta, P Yarramsetti, S Kethavath
Proceedings of International Conference on Computational Intelligence and Data Engineering (ICCIDE), 2020
Springer / Google Scholar
This paper provides a detailed analysis of various deep learning methodologies for forecasting PM2.5 levels, addressing critical environmental challenges through advanced AI techniques.

Towards Imperceptible Adversarial Image Generation: Minimizing Perceptual Difference

Sivaji Retta, R Srinivasan
25th IEEE International Symposium on Multimedia, 2023
IEEE / Google Scholar
This research introduces novel techniques for generating imperceptible adversarial images, focusing on minimizing perceptual differences to enhance image security in digital media.

MTYOLO: A Multi-Task Model to Concurrently Obtain the Vital Characteristics of Individuals or Animals

KE Ong, Sivaji Retta, R Srinivasan, S Tan, J Liu
2024 IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo Workshops (ICMEW), 2024
IEEE / Google Scholar
MTYOLO presents a multi-task model aimed at concurrently determining vital characteristics in both individuals and animals, leveraging advanced deep learning techniques.

CattleDeSegNet: A Joint Approach to Cattle Denoising and Interpretable Segmentation

Sivaji Retta, R Srinivasan, S Tan
16th SPIE International Conference on Machine Vision, 2023
SPIE Digital Library / Google Scholar
CattleDeSegNet introduces a joint model that combines cattle denoising and interpretable segmentation, addressing key challenges in livestock monitoring through AI.
